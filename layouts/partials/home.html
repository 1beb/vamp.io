Build, deploy and manage microservices with power and ease.

Vamp, or the Very Awesome Microservices Platform, takes the pain out of running complex and critical service based architectures. Vamp's core features are a platform-agnostic microservices DSL, A-B testing/canary releasing on everything and a deep metrics & event engine that monitors everything and directly feeds back into your services.
We are currently in alpha but you can already start kicking the tires!

Manage all services in your topology, as a topology.

Complex service architectures have complex dependencies and specific requirements when it comes to managing versions, performance, scale and availability. Vamp gives you the tools to manage service chains of arbitrary complexity and control the scale, health, version and topology of your services in real time.

Easy A/B testing and Canary Releasing on everything

Vamp has a deeply ingrained support for A/B-testing, Canary and Blue/Green releases. Vamp's DSL and API has full support for many common (and not so common) scenarios for either bringing your changes to production safely or just trying out new things without the associated risks.

Transition from 0% to 100%

Manually adjusting load balancers or web servers on every release? Vamp gives you a straight DSL and API to plan your canary releases, blue/green deployments and A/B tests.

Define test groups with filters

Test out a new service with just your IOS users? Expose a service to specific part of your company? Set up simple (or complex) test groups with the DSL or the API.

Platform agnostic microservices DSL

Using Vamp blueprints, you specify all of your dependencies and runtime behaviour, like what traffic should go where and what SLA levels your service should stick to. Think platform independent AWS Cloudformation with added fairy dust.

name: my-blueprint
  endpoints:
    frontend.ports.port: $PORT
clusters:
  frontend:
    services:
      -
        breed: apache/final
        scale:
          cpu: 0.2
          memory: 120
          instances: 2
        routing:
          weight: 95
      -
        breed: apache/experimental
        scale:
          cpu: 0.2
          memory: 120
          instances: 2
        routing:
          weight: 5
          filters:
            - condition: user-agent = android
    sla:
      type: sliding_window
      metric: response_time
        threshold:
          upper: 1000
          lower: 100
        window:
          interval: 10
          cooldown: 10
        reset:
          time: 20
      escalation:
        scale_instances:
          minimum: 1
          maximum: 3
          scale_by: 1
